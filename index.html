Personal (Sound) Space

An installation project developed for the “Perspective in Design” module (Hyperspace) in the Design and Computation master's program at TU Berlin and UdK Berlin. “Personal (Sound) Space” explores how sound can define and protect personal space in a public environment, inviting participants to reconsider the boundaries of spatial perception through auditory cues.

About me

Eman is currently pursuing a master’s degree in “Design and Computation” at the Berlin University of the Arts (UdK) and the Technical University (TU) in Berlin. As an interdisciplinary artist, he combines music, technology, and design to craft innovative soundscapes and interactive installations.

With a bachelor’s degree in chemical engineering, Eman brings a systematic and experimental perspective to his creative work. He is a musician, guitar teacher, and sound engineer specializing in electronic music, generative music, and sound installations. Always eager to push the boundaries of conventional music and sound design, Eman aspires to create immersive experiences that resonate with audiences on multiple levels.
